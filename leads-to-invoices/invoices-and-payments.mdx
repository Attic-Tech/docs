The final stage of the pipeline turns delivered work into cash. An **invoice** bills the client, **payments** record what they pay, and **credit notes** handle any refunds or adjustments — closing the loop that started with a lead.

## Invoices

An invoice is generated for the work — most often **from the estimate**, so the billed amount matches what was sold. It can also be created as a standalone manual invoice.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build">
    Invoices carry **line items** (description, quantity, unit price, total) and roll up to a **subtotal**, **tax**, and **total**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Number & dates">
    A unique invoice **number** is generated automatically. The invoice has an **issue date**, a **due date**, and an optional **payment term** (such as _Due on Receipt_ or _Net 30_).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    Sending records a **send date**, and the system tracks whether the client has **viewed** it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Invoice status

An invoice's status is derived from its **balance** — the total minus payments applied to it.

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Draft | Created but not yet sent |
| Open | Sent, nothing paid yet |
| Partially Paid | Some payment received, balance remaining |
| Paid | Balance is zero |
| Voided | Cancelled, with a recorded reason |

## Payments and allocation

A **payment** records money received — its amount, method (card, ACH, cash, and so on), and any reference number. Payments aren't tied blindly to a single bill: a **payment allocation** maps each payment to one or more invoices.

<Info>
  Allocation is what lets one payment cover several invoices, or a deposit be split across bills. An invoice becomes **Paid** only when allocations bring its balance to zero.
</Info>

## Credit notes & adjustments

When a refund, courtesy discount, or correction is needed, a **credit note** records it with a reason code. Like payments, credit notes are **allocated** to invoices to reduce the balance owed. A credit note can itself be voided, with that reversal tracked for a clean audit trail.

<Warning>
  Use credit notes — not edits to a sent invoice — for adjustments after an invoice goes out. This preserves the original billed record and keeps your accounting auditable.
</Warning>

## Closing the loop

When an invoice is fully **Paid**, the originating **job** can move to **Closed**. The journey that began with a single lead ends with collected, reconciled revenue.
