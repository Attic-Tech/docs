A **job** is the operational record for delivering authorized work. It carries the work through crew assignment, planning, scheduling, and execution — all the way to completion and payment. A job is created from a work order and references the originating estimate, so scope and cost stay traceable.

## The job plan

Each job has one **job plan** — the crew lead's detailed blueprint for getting the work done.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Draft">
    The crew lead builds the plan: breaking work into **plan days**, and assigning items and materials to each day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete">
    The plan is submitted for review. The submission time is stamped automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approved">
    A reviewer approves the plan, recording who approved it and when. The job is now ready to schedule.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Before submitting, the crew lead acknowledges that they've read the work-order **crew notes** — ensuring on-site context isn't missed.
</Note>

## Job status lifecycle

The job's own status tracks the bigger operational picture, from needing a crew lead all the way to closed.

| Status | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| Requires Crew Lead | New job, no crew lead assigned yet |
| Plans In Progress | Crew lead is building the plan |
| Pending Review | Plan submitted, awaiting approval |
| Requires Scheduling | Plan approved, needs calendar slots |
| Pre-Production | Scheduled; crew is prepping |
| In Production | Work is actively underway |
| Production Complete | Work finished, awaiting payment |
| Pending Payment | Invoiced, payment outstanding |
| Closed Job | Fully complete and paid |
| Cancelled | Job will not proceed |

<Warning>
  If a job is sent back to **Requires Crew Lead**, its crew assignments and shifts are cleared — the plan has to be re-staffed from a clean slate.
</Warning>

## Scheduling & crew

Approved plans are placed on the calendar as **shifts**, with crew, vehicles, and equipment requested as needed. The **scheduled date** is set as the job moves into Pre-Production, and a **closed date** is stamped automatically when the job closes — which also feeds crew bonus calculations.

<Tip>
  Track **actual material cost** against the estimate as the job runs. The variance between quoted and actual cost is a key signal for job profitability.
</Tip>

## From completed job to invoice

Once work is delivered, the job is billed. The invoice draws on the same project and estimate, closing the loop between what was sold and what gets collected.
