An **estimate** is the priced quote for a project at a property. It defines the scope, the line items, the pricing, and the proposed payment terms — and it is the record that, once accepted, authorizes work to begin.

## Building an estimate

Estimates are assembled from your **service catalog** — reusable items organized into categories — so pricing stays consistent across quotes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add scope">
    Pull **items** from the catalog and set quantities. Items carry units, costs, and categories.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Price it">
    The estimate tracks **retail cost**, **true cost**, and a **final price**, plus labor hours and tax.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set terms">
    Choose a **payment method** (cash, credit, or financing) and a **deposit method**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Snapshot">
    On creation the estimate stores an immutable **snapshot** of items and pricing, so the quoted numbers are preserved even if the catalog changes later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Estimate status lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step title="In Progress">
    The estimate is a draft — fully editable while the salesperson refines scope and price.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sold">
    The customer has accepted. The first time an estimate is sold is stamped permanently for sales reporting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Released">
    Work is authorized to proceed, which kicks off the **work order** and **job**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Other states include **Secondary Estimate** (an alternative option offered alongside the primary quote), **Lost** (the customer declined), and **Deleted**.

| Status | Editable? | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| In Progress | Yes | Draft being built |
| Sold | Locked | Customer accepted |
| Released | Locked | Authorized — work begins |
| Secondary Estimate | Yes | Alternative quote |
| Lost | Locked | Customer declined |

<Note>
  **Crew notes** stay editable even after an estimate is locked, so operations can add context for the crew without reopening pricing.
</Note>

<Tip>
  The _first sold_ date is captured automatically and used for commission and sales metrics. Only branch management can adjust the effective sold date — this keeps reporting trustworthy.
</Tip>

## What happens after Sold

A sold estimate can be turned into a formal **proposal** for the customer to sign, or it can move straight to **Released** to authorize work.
