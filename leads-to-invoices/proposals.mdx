A **proposal** is the polished, customer-facing version of an estimate. It presents the quote, the payment options, and a place for the customer to sign — formalizing the agreement before work begins. Proposals are optional, but they create a clean paper trail and capture the customer's chosen payment terms.

<Note>
  A proposal is tied one-to-one to an estimate. Each estimate can have a single proposal, built from the estimate's snapshot so the customer sees exactly what was quoted.
</Note>

## The proposal lifecycle

Rather than a single status field, a proposal advances through a series of timestamps that record what the customer did and when.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Draft">
    The proposal is being prepared and has not been sent. You can add a personalized **note to the client** and choose which salesperson's contact details appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sent">
    The proposal is emailed to the customer. An **expiration date** sets the deadline to accept.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Opened">
    The system records when the customer first views the proposal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Signed">
    The customer accepts, capturing their **signature** and the **payment method / financing term** they selected. This authorizes work to proceed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Other outcomes

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Changes requested" icon="pen-to-square">
    The customer asks for revisions. The estimate can be updated and a fresh proposal sent.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Rejected" icon="circle-xmark">
    The customer declines, with a recorded **rejection reason**. The underlying estimate may be re-quoted or marked Lost.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Expired" icon="clock">
    The acceptance deadline passes without a signature. Re-send with a new expiration to revive it.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What the customer chooses

The estimate can offer more than one way to pay. When the customer signs, their selections are saved on the proposal:

- **Selected payment method** — e.g. cash, credit card, or financing
- **Selected financing term** — e.g. 3, 6, or 12 months, when financing is chosen

These choices flow forward into invoicing.

<Tip>
  Set an expiration that creates healthy urgency without rushing the customer. A signed proposal is the cleanest trigger for kicking off the work order and job.
</Tip>
