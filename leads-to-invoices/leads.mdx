A **lead** is a prospective customer who has shown interest in your services but hasn't yet become a paying client. Leads are where the pipeline begins — sales reps work them from first contact through qualification.

## What a lead captures

Every lead is built around a **primary contact** and is attributed to a **source** so you can measure where business comes from.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Contact details">
    A required **primary contact** (name, email, phone) plus any **secondary contacts** for the household or business.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Location">
    The service address is captured and automatically geocoded to map coordinates for routing and territory views.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attribution">
    A **lead source** (for example _Google Ads_, _Referral_, or _Cold Call_) records how the prospect found you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ownership">
    A **salesperson** and a **client coordinator** are assigned so follow-up has a clear owner.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Lead status

Each lead carries a **status** that reflects where it sits in qualification. Status values are configurable per workspace, but they typically follow a path like:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| New | Just captured, not yet worked |
| Contacted | Sales has made first contact |
| Qualified | Confirmed as a real opportunity |
| Won | Accepted — ready to convert to a client |
| Lost | Not moving forward |

A **confidence level** lets the salesperson signal how likely the lead is to close, which feeds sales forecasting.

## Activity history

Every meaningful touch — calls, emails, status changes — is recorded as a **lead activity**, giving a timeline of how the relationship has progressed. This history stays attached to the lead so anyone picking it up has full context.

<Tip>
  Keep the source field accurate. Lead-source attribution is what powers your marketing ROI and cost-per-lead reporting later on.
</Tip>

## Converting a lead

When a lead is qualified and the customer is ready to move forward, it is converted into a **client** account. The primary contact carries over, and you can begin registering the **properties** where work will be performed.
