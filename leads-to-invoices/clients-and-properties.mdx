Once a lead is won, it becomes a **client** — a lasting customer account that holds billing details and contacts. Each client owns one or more **properties**, the physical locations where your crews actually do the work.

## Clients

A client is the billing and relationship hub for everything that follows.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Contacts">
    A required **primary contact**, plus optional **secondary contacts** for shared decision-makers or tenants.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Billing address">
    A dedicated billing address (which may differ from the service location) determines where invoices are sent.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Relationship">
    A **client since** date and free-form account **notes** keep history and context in one place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Payments">
    A linked payment-provider customer record lets you charge cards and reconcile payments later in the pipeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  One client can have many properties. A landlord, a property-management company, or a homeowner with multiple addresses all map cleanly to a single client with several properties.
</Note>

## Properties

A **property** is a specific service location tied to exactly one client. Properties carry the on-site detail crews need to show up prepared.

| Field | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Address | Geocoded to map coordinates for routing |
| Property type | e.g. single-family home, apartment |
| Stories & parking | Helps crews plan access and equipment |
| Occupied | Whether someone will be present |
| Hazards | Safety flags for the crew |
| Concerning pets | Crew-safety heads-up |
| Special instructions | Gate codes, access notes, preferences |

<Warning>
  Accurate property details — especially hazards, pets, and access instructions — directly affect crew safety and first-visit success. Capture them up front.
</Warning>
