When AI is turned on for your branch, the Calculator can review your estimate and flag anything that looks missing or out of place — like an item it would expect given the work you've described. It's a quick second set of eyes before you save or send.

<Note>
These are suggestions to help you catch gaps, not blockers. You decide what to act on.
</Note>

## Run the review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the check">
    On the breakdown, look for **AI: Check for issues**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run it">
    Tap it. While it works, you'll see **Checking estimate…**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the result">
    If everything looks complete, you'll get a green **Estimate looks good**. If not, you'll see an **AI Review** with a count of how many issues it found.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Work through the warnings

Each issue appears as its own note, color-coded by how much it matters:

- **Errors** point to something that looks clearly wrong or missing.
- **Warnings** flag something worth a second look.
- **Info** notes are gentle, "you might also consider" suggestions.

For each note:

- **Make the fix** in the estimate if the AI caught a real gap — add the missing item, adjust a quantity, and so on.
- **Dismiss** it with the **×** if you've considered it and it doesn't apply. Dismissing only hides that note; it doesn't change your estimate.

## Re-check after changes

After you make changes, choose **Re-check** to run the review again. The AI looks at the updated estimate and gives you a fresh result — ideally the green **Estimate looks good**.

<Tip>
Run the check once your work areas and items are mostly in place. Running it on a nearly finished estimate gives the AI the full picture and the most useful feedback.
</Tip>
