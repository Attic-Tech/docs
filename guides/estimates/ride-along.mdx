The **AI Ride Along** records your in-person sales conversation so you can focus on the customer instead of taking notes. When you're done, AI analyzes the conversation and gives you coaching insights to help you sharpen your next pitch.

<Note>
You'll be asked for **microphone permission** the first time you record. The recording keeps running even if your screen dims, and there's a maximum length — long conversations stop on their own when they reach the limit.
</Note>

## Record the conversation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start recording">
    On the **AI Ride Along** screen, tap the large **microphone** button. Allow microphone access if your device asks. The status changes to **Recording…** and a timer starts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch the indicator">
    A glowing ring pulses around the button in time with the sound it's picking up — a quick way to confirm it's hearing you. The timer shows how long you've been recording.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pause if you need to">
    Tap **Pause** (or the button itself) to take a break — for paperwork, a phone call, or a private moment. Tap **Resume** to pick back up. The timer holds while you're paused.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop when you're finished">
    Tap **Stop Recording**. The recording uploads and the status moves through **Uploading…** and **Starting analysis…**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let AI take over">
    Once it shows **Complete**, the recording is in and AI analysis is underway. You can start another recording, or head **Back to Estimate**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## If something goes wrong

If a recording fails — most often because microphone access was denied — you'll see a short message explaining what happened.

- If you denied microphone access, allow it in your browser or device settings, then try again.
- Use **Try Again** to start a fresh recording, or **Cancel** to step away.

<Tip>
For the cleanest analysis, keep the device near the conversation and minimize background noise. If you get interrupted, **Pause** rather than stop — that keeps everything in one recording.
</Tip>
