title: "Change an Estimate's Status"
description: "Mark estimates Sold, Lost, Released, and more — right from the Estimates list."

An estimate's **status** tells you and your team where it stands — still being worked on, won, lost, and so on. You can change it directly from the **Estimates** list, no need to reopen the Calculator.

## The statuses

- **In Progress** — you're still building or editing it. This is the only status where the estimate's items and pricing can be changed.
- **Sold** — the customer said yes. This is how your team tracks which estimates turned into real, won work.
- **Released** — handed off (for example, to be scheduled or fulfilled).
- **Lost** — the customer didn't move forward.
- **Secondary Estimate** — an alternate option for the same job that isn't the primary quote.

## Change the status

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the estimate">
    Open the **Estimates** list and locate the row you want. (On a phone, use the estimate's card.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the status menu">
    Click the **status badge** in the **Status** column. A menu of available statuses opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the new status">
    Choose the status you want. The change saves right away and the badge updates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Which statuses you can choose depends on your role. If an option isn't available to you, it won't appear as a choice.
</Note>

## Marking an estimate Sold

When the customer accepts, set the estimate to **Sold**. Once it's sold, a **Sold Date** appears on the row. If you ever need to correct when the deal actually closed, you can adjust that date right on the sold estimate.

## Moving a Sold estimate back

Moving an estimate **out of Sold** is a bigger deal, so it's more controlled — typically it requires manager-level permission. If you don't have it, the option won't be available.

## Editing again after a status change

Only **In Progress** estimates can have their items and pricing edited. If you need to change the work on an estimate that's already Sold or in another status, switch it back to **In Progress** first.

<Note>
If the estimate's proposal has already been **signed** by the customer, moving it back to In Progress will need to clear that signature so the estimate can be edited again. You'll be asked to confirm before that happens — and you'll see a confirmation once the signature has been removed.
</Note>
