When the AI Calculator is turned on for your branch, you can hand it property photos instead of a written description. The AI looks at what's in the pictures and turns it into a starting estimate you can review and refine.

<Note>
AI gives you a head start, not the final word. Always review the breakdown it produces before you save or send anything.
</Note>

## Before you start

On the first step of the Calculator, choose the **Client Name** and the **project** this estimate belongs to. The AI builder needs both before it can generate anything.

## Add your photos

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Photos tab">
    On the AI builder, switch from **Describe** to the **Photos** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add up to 10 photos">
    In the **Upload property photos** area you can **Browse files** to pick photos from your device, or **Take photo** to snap one with your phone's camera. Add as many as you need, up to **10**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove any you don't want">
    Each photo shows a small thumbnail. Hover (or tap) a thumbnail and use the **×** to remove it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add context (optional)">
    Use the notes box to tell the AI anything the photos don't show — for example, *"this is a 2-story home, steep pitch roof."* This is optional but helps it get the details right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the estimate">
    Choose **Analyze Photos and Generate Estimate**. While it works, the button shows how many photos it's analyzing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Review what comes back

Once the AI finishes, it drops a description of what it saw into the chat and builds the estimate from it — the same **Estimate Generated** card you'd get from a typed description, listing the items in each work area. Read it over, then choose **Accept & Review Breakdown** to open the full summary and fine-tune anything before saving.

<Tip>
If something didn't come out right, switch back to the **Describe** tab and chat with the assistant to adjust it — *"add a ridge vent"* or *"this attic is closer to 1,800 sqft."*
</Tip>
