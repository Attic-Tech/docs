A **proposal** is the customer-facing version of an estimate. It takes the work you priced in the Calculator and presents it as a clean, branded document the customer can read, review, and sign online. You start it from the estimate's breakdown and then personalize it before sending.

<Note>
Proposals are available when they're turned on for your branch. If you don't see the **Generate Proposal** button, your branch isn't set up for proposals yet — check with your manager.
</Note>

## Before you start

You generate a proposal from a finished estimate. Build and save the estimate in the Calculator first, all the way to the **Overall Job Breakdown** step. The proposal pulls its services, prices, photos, and branding directly from that breakdown, so the more complete the estimate, the better the proposal looks.

<Tip>
You don't have to mark the estimate **Sold** to create a proposal. In fact, the proposal is usually what gets the customer to say yes — you send it, they sign, and then you mark the estimate Sold.
</Tip>

## Generate the proposal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the estimate's breakdown">
    Open the estimate in the Calculator and go to the final **Overall Job Breakdown** step. This is the complete, itemized summary with the calculated total.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Generate Proposal">
    On the breakdown, press **Generate Proposal**. Menaia builds the proposal from the estimate and opens it for you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Land on the Proposal Preview">
    You're taken to the **Proposal Preview** — the editable, customer-facing layout of the proposal. This is where you personalize it before sending.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The **Generate Proposal** button only appears the first time. Once a proposal exists for that estimate, the button becomes **View Proposal**, so you can reopen the proposal any time instead of creating a duplicate.
</Note>


## What's on the Proposal Preview

The preview is the proposal as the customer will see it, with a few editable areas just for you. From top to bottom you'll find:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Header and branding">
    Your company logo and details, plus a **Sent Date** once the proposal has been sent. At the top of the page, a status badge reflects the underlying estimate's state.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="To / From">
    The **To** block shows the customer and the job address. The **From** block shows the salesperson whose name and contact details appear on the proposal — you can change who that is from a dropdown here.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Notes to Client">
    A personal message to the customer. It starts empty — add one to make the proposal feel tailored. (See **Edit the note and price display**.)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="About, reviews, and media">
    Your company's "about" section, customer reviews for your branch, and any photos or videos you attach to the proposal or to individual line items.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Project Allowances">
    An optional section for custom terms, allowances, or notes about the work. It starts empty — add one when you want to spell out something specific for the customer. (See **Edit the note and price display**.)
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Service Breakdown">
    The itemized list of work, grouped by work area. You control whether unit and total prices are shown to the customer here.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Price Summary">
    Subtotal, any discount, taxes, total, required deposit, and the payment options the customer can choose from (including financing, when offered).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Set who the proposal is from

In the **From** block, use the salesperson dropdown to choose whose name, phone, and contact details appear on the proposal. It defaults to the person who created the estimate, which is usually exactly right — change it only if someone else should be the customer's point of contact.

## Attach photos and videos

To make the proposal more convincing, you can add media:

- **Proposal-level media** — photos or videos for the job as a whole.
- **Line-item media** — attach photos or videos to a specific service so the customer can see exactly what that work involves. Look for **Attach photos or videos for this line item** under each item, then **Upload Media**.

<Tip>
Media you add here only shows on the proposal — it doesn't change the estimate's pricing.
</Tip>

## Save your work

Your personalization is saved as you go, and you can press **Save as Draft** at any time to keep the proposal as a draft without sending it. A draft is fully editable and the customer can't see it yet.
