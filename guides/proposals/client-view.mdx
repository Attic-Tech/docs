When you send a proposal, your customer gets an email with a secure link. Clicking it opens the proposal in their browser — no login, no app, no password. This page walks through exactly what they experience, so you can speak to it with confidence on a sales call.

## Opening the proposal

The customer clicks the link in their email and the proposal opens to a friendly greeting — **Hi, [first name]** — with the prompt *"View your custom proposal below and take action."* They can scroll through the whole thing and **print** it if they'd like a paper copy.

<Note>
The moment a customer first opens the proposal, you're notified that they viewed it. (See **Handle the customer's response**.)
</Note>

## Reviewing the work

As the customer scrolls, they see the proposal you built, in order:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Your branding and a personal note">
    Your company header, the **To / From** details, and your **Notes to Client** message if you added one.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="About you and your reviews">
    Your company's "about" section and customer reviews for your branch — social proof, right where they're deciding.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Photos and videos">
    Any media you attached, both for the job overall and on individual services.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Project Allowances">
    Any custom terms, allowances, or notes you added for this proposal. This section only appears if you filled it in.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Service Breakdown">
    The itemized work, grouped by area. Whether they see per-item prices depends on the **Unit Price** and **Total Price** choices you made when building the proposal.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Price Summary">
    Subtotal, any discount, taxes, the total, and the **Required Deposit**.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Comparing competing quotes (WisePick)

When **WisePick** is enabled for your branch, the customer also sees a WisePick card near the top of the proposal inviting them to compare quotes:

- They click **Compare Quotes** and upload up to 3 competing proposals as PDF files — no login needed.
- WisePick analyzes the documents side by side (it usually takes 15–30 seconds), comparing pricing, materials, scope of work, terms and conditions, credentials, warranty, and overall quality.
- When it's done, **View Comparison** opens the side-by-side analysis, and they can reopen it any time.

<Note>
When a customer uploads competing proposals, your team is notified and can see the same analysis and the uploaded documents — see **Handle the customer's response**.
</Note>

## Choosing a payment option

In the **Price Summary**, under **Payment Options**, the customer picks how they'd like to pay from the options your branch offers. If **financing** is one of them, selecting it reveals more:

- A **Loan Term** dropdown, with your most common term marked **Most Popular**.
- An estimated **monthly payment** and approximate **APR** for the term they pick.
- An **Apply Now** link to your financing partner, when one is set up.

<Note>
Financing figures shown here are **estimates only and subject to credit approval** — the proposal says so right on the page. They help the customer picture the monthly cost; they're not a final offer.
</Note>

The payment option the customer selects is recorded with their approval, so your team knows how they intend to pay.

## Signing the proposal

When the customer is ready to accept, they sign right on the page in the **Sign Proposal** section:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the agreement line">
    Above the signature area: *"By signing below, I acknowledge that I have read and agree to the terms and conditions outlined in this proposal."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the name">
    They type the signer's full name in **Client Name** (pre-filled with the customer's name). Today's date fills in automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign">
    They draw their signature in the signature box with a finger, stylus, or mouse. A **Clear** button lets them redo it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve">
    With a name and signature in place, they tap **Sign & Approve** (or **Approve Proposal**). Both a name and a drawn signature are required — the approve button stays unavailable until both are there.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The customer sees a confirmation that their approval was recorded, and the action bar updates to show **Approved Proposal**. The signature, the signer's name, and the date are saved on the proposal as a record.

## Their other options

If the customer isn't ready to approve, the action bar at the bottom gives them two more choices (on mobile these live under **Other Options**):

- **Request Changes** — ask for an adjustment and describe what they'd like changed.
- **Reject Proposal** — decline, optionally with a reason and a note.

Both send their feedback straight to your sales team. What happens after each of these is covered in **Handle the customer's response**.
