When your proposal looks the way you want, you send it to the customer by email. They receive a **secure link** that opens the proposal in their browser, where they can review it, choose a payment option, and sign — no account or password needed.

## Send the proposal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Send Proposal">
    On the **Proposal Preview**, click **Send Proposal**. A window opens titled **Email proposal to [customer name]**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the recipients">
    The customer's email is filled in for you when it's on file. Add more people with **Add Recipient** (for example, a spouse or business partner), or remove a row with the trash icon. Everyone you list gets their own secure link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the subject and message">
    The **Subject** and **Body** come pre-filled from your branch's template, with the customer's name, your name, your phone number, and the date already merged in. Edit them however you like.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Polish the message with AI (optional)">
    Click **Enhance with AI** to have Menaia rewrite your message into a clean, professional version. Don't like it? Use the revert option to put your original wording back.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    Click **Send Proposal**. Menaia emails each recipient a secure link. You'll see a confirmation that the proposal was sent.
  </Step>
</Steps>


<Note>
If you start from a brand-new preview and haven't saved a draft yet, sending creates the proposal automatically — you don't need to save first.
</Note>

## What sending does

Sending the proposal kicks off a few things at once:

- Each recipient gets an **email with a secure link** to view and sign the proposal.
- The proposal is stamped with a **Sent Date**, which now shows in the header.
- The estimate it's based on moves to a **Released** state, so your team can see it's out with the customer and awaiting a response.

<Tip>
You can keep working on most of the proposal after sending. If you make changes you want the customer to see, just resend (see below) so they get an up-to-date link.
</Tip>

## Resend a proposal

After a proposal has been sent, the **Send Proposal** button becomes **Resend Proposal**. Use it to email a fresh link — handy if the customer lost the original email, you added a recipient, or you updated the proposal.

<Note>
Resending issues a new secure link and clears any earlier response (such as a request for changes) so the customer is looking at the current version. Their previously recorded signature is not affected by resending.
</Note>

## Expiration: how the secure link stays safe

The link you email is a **secure, time-limited link**. After a period of time it **expires** automatically — this protects the customer's information so an old email link can't be opened by just anyone later.

You don't set an expiration date yourself; it's built into the secure link. Here's what that means in practice:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What the customer sees when a link expires">
    If the customer clicks an expired link, they land on a friendly page that says **This proposal link has expired** and explains the link expired to protect their information. When self-service is available, they can request a new link right there and it's emailed to them immediately.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Getting the customer a fresh link">
    Either the customer requests a new link from the expired page, or you simply click **Resend Proposal** to email them a new one. The proposal contents are unchanged — only the access link is refreshed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="If a signature is already recorded">
    A proposal the customer has already signed stays signed. An expired *link* only affects opening the page again; it doesn't undo a completed signature.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Present to the customer in person

Sitting with the customer? After a proposal has been sent at least once, open the **More actions** menu (or the **Present to Client** button on mobile) and choose **Present to Client**. This opens the live customer-facing proposal right on your device, so you can walk through it together and even capture the signature on the spot.
