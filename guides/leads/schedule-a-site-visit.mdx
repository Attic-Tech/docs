A **lead activity** is a visit booked on the calendar for one of your leads — usually a site visit so a team member can go look at the property. Scheduling one puts the appointment on the calendar, assigns who's going, and (when you're ready) notifies the team and the customer.

## Before you start

A lead needs a few things in place before it can be scheduled. The lead must have a **first name**, **last name**, an **address**, **either a phone or an email**, and a **branch** assigned.

<Note>
If any of these are missing, the **Schedule Lead Activity** button is greyed out, and a tooltip tells you exactly what's needed. Fill in the gaps first — see [Create a lead](/guides/leads/create-a-lead) and [Qualify a lead](/guides/leads/qualify-a-lead).
</Note>

## Start scheduling

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lead">
    Go to **Leads** and click the lead you want to visit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Schedule Lead Activity">
    At the top-right of the lead, press **Schedule Lead Activity**. This takes you to the calendar with the **Schedule New Lead Activity** panel open, already pointed at this lead.
  </Step>
</Steps>


## Fill in the visit

The scheduling panel pre-fills what it can from the lead — you just confirm and finish.

- **Lead Name** — the lead this visit is for. It's already selected, but you can switch to another lead, or use **Create New Lead** if you need one on the spot.
- **Phone Number** and **Email** — pulled from the lead's contact; adjust if needed.
- **Address** — the property address, copied from the lead. Retype to change it.
- **Project Name** — a name for the work, pre-filled from the lead. Edit it to whatever's clearest.
- **Assign To** — the team member going on the visit. Search and pick someone (if you can only assign yourself, this shows you automatically).
- **Date**, **Start Time**, and **End Time** — when the visit happens.
- **Lead notes** — copied from the lead so the assignee has context; edit freely without changing the lead itself.
- **Client Confirmed** — check this once the customer has confirmed the appointment.

<Tip>
If you pick a time that clashes with another appointment for the same person, the system warns you about the conflict before booking, so you can adjust.
</Tip>

## Save as draft or publish

Two buttons at the bottom decide what happens next:

- **Save Draft** — books the visit but keeps it as a draft, so you can finish the details later. Nobody is notified yet.
- **Publish Activity** — confirms the visit. Before it saves, you'll choose who to notify — the **assigned team** and the **lead's email** — and can add extra email recipients.

Either way, the visit appears on the calendar and in the lead's **Upcoming Events** list.

<Note>
Publishing a lead activity is also the moment a qualified lead becomes a client and project. See [Turn a lead into a client](/guides/leads/turn-lead-into-client) for what happens next.
</Note>

## Manage a scheduled visit

Open any lead activity from the calendar (or from the lead's **Upcoming Events**) to review or change it. From there you can update the time, reassign it, or **Delete** it — when deleting, you can optionally notify the assigned user and the lead contact.
