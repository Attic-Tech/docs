A **lead** is a potential customer you're working toward winning. Creating one is the first step in your pipeline: you capture who they are, where the property is, and how they found you. Once a lead exists, you can qualify it, schedule a site visit, and eventually turn it into a client.

<Note>
You only need a little to get started — a last name and a phone number is enough to save a lead. You can always fill in the rest later.
</Note>

## Start a new lead

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Leads page">
    Go to **Leads** from the main navigation. This is where every lead lives — you can search, filter, and reopen any of them here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New Lead">
    Press the **New Lead** button in the top-right corner. The **Create New Lead** form opens. (If your list is empty, you'll see a **Create a new lead to get started** prompt that does the same thing.)
  </Step>
</Steps>


## Fill in the lead details

The form is grouped into a few simple sections. Work top to bottom.

### Copy from an existing client (optional)

At the very top is **Copy from existing client**. If this person is already a customer, search for them by name, email, or phone and pick them — the form auto-fills their contact and address details so you don't have to retype anything. If they're brand new, just skip this and fill the form in yourself.

<Tip>
As you type a phone or email, the system quietly checks whether a matching client already exists. If it finds one, you'll see a green **Existing client found, auto-filled details** banner and the details fill in for you.
</Tip>

### Contact Details

This is the person you'll be talking to — the primary contact for the lead.

- **First Name** and **Last Name** — the contact's name. (Last name is required.)
- **Phone** and **Email** — a phone number is required; email is optional but worth capturing.
- **Relationship to Property** — how this person relates to the property (for example, the homeowner or a tenant).

The first contact you enter is the **Primary Contact**, marked with a switch at the top of the card. You can add more people later — see [Edit a lead and add contacts](/guides/leads/edit-and-contacts).

### Property Details

Enter the **Address** of the property. Start typing and pick the match from the suggestions — the city, state, and ZIP fill in automatically, and the lead gets pinned correctly on the map.

### Additional Details

- **Confidence Level** — how likely you think this lead is to close: **Low**, **Medium**, or **High**.
- **Lead source** — where the lead came from. See [Set the lead source, confidence, and referral](/guides/leads/source-confidence-referral) for what to expect when a source asks for referral info.
- **Lead notes** — anything useful about the lead. Optional, but a good place for context the rest of the team should see.

## Save it

When you're done, click **Create Lead** at the bottom. You'll see a **Lead created successfully** confirmation, and the new lead appears in your Leads list, ready to work.

<Tip>
If something's missing, the form points it out before saving. The most common nudge is a contact that still needs a last name and a phone number.
</Tip>
