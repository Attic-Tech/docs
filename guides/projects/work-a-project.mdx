A **project** is the home base for one job at one address. It pulls together everything that happens for that customer — who you're working with, where the work is, the estimate, the job, scheduled visits, photos, forms, and billing — so you don't have to chase information across the app.

<Note>
Think of a project as the file folder for a single customer's work at a single property. Open it once and everything related is a tab away.
</Note>

## What a project pulls together

Every project is tied to one **client** (the customer) and one **property** (the address where the work happens). From there it links the rest of the work for that address:

- The **estimate** built for the job (and any alternates).
- The **job** that gets created when an estimate is sold.
- The **contacts** to reach about this work.
- The **lead** the project came from, when there is one.
- All the **media, forms, schedule, and invoices** for the work.

<Tip>
One project, one address. If the same client needs work at a second property, that's a second project.
</Tip>

## Open a project

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the client">
    Go to **Clients** from the main navigation and open the customer you're working with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the project">
    From the client, open the project for the address you're working on. The project opens on its **Overview** tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>


## Find your way around

A project is organized into tabs along the top. You'll always see **Overview**, and the rest appear depending on what your branch has turned on and what's happened on the project so far.

| Tab | What's there |
| --- | --- |
| **Overview** | The summary screen — contact details, property details, lead information, assignment, and shortcuts. |
| **Estimates** | The estimate(s) and proposal(s) for this job. |
| **Schedule** | Visits and appointments booked for this work. |
| **Job Plan** | The plan the crew works from. |
| **Media** | Photos, videos, and documents for the project. See [Add & manage project media](/guides/projects/media). |
| **Comments** | The running conversation about this project. |
| **Forms** | Forms filled out for the job. |
| **Invoices** & **Financial Summary** | Billing and payment tracking for the work. |

<Note>
If you don't see a tab, it isn't part of your branch's setup or there's nothing for it yet — for example, **Estimates** and the billing tabs only appear once those features are enabled for you.
</Note>

## What you see on the Overview

The Overview is split into a main column and a sidebar.

**In the main column:**

- **Contact Details** — the primary contact and any secondary contacts for the work. See [Edit project details & contacts](/guides/projects/edit-details).
- **Property Details** — the address, property type, and parking. The address links out to maps, and shows the driving distance from your branch when it can be worked out.
- **Lead Information** — where the project came from, shown when the project started from a lead.

**In the sidebar:**

- **Upcoming Events** — the next visits and appointments, when scheduling is turned on.
- **Assignment** — the **Sales Person** credited with the work and the **Crew Lead** assigned to the job.
- **Shortcuts** — quick links to the estimate, when one exists.

<Tip>
The **Sales Person** is filled in for you automatically based on the most recent lead activity — they're the person credited with the sale in **My Performance**.
</Tip>

## Let the customer know you're on the way

When you open a project you'll see a **Send On My Way Text** button near **Create New** at the top of the page. Use it to send the customer a quick text message letting them know your team is heading to their location. On a smaller screen the same action lives in the **⋯** menu in the header.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the sender">
    Click **Send On My Way Text**. The **Send an On My Way text** window opens with the project's contacts ready to choose from.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick who to text">
    Choose the contact to notify — the **primary contact** is selected for you, and you can switch to any secondary contact. If the contact you pick has no phone number on file, add a mobile number; it's saved to that contact so it's there next time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the message">
    A message is filled in for you — it greets the contact by name and names your company. Edit it however you like; a character count shows how much room is left.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send it">
    Click **Send now**. The text goes out to the customer right away, and you'll see an **On My Way text sent** confirmation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
After a text goes out, the button shows **Sent** with the time it was last sent for this project, so you can tell at a glance whether the customer has already been notified.
</Note>

<Tip>
Sending an On My Way text is available to **sales roles and above** — the same people who can edit a project's details.
</Tip>

## How a project moves forward

A project doesn't have a status of its own — its story is told by the work inside it:

<Steps>
  <Step title="It starts from a lead or a new estimate">
    Most projects begin when you build an estimate for a customer at an address. If the work came in as a lead, the project carries that lead's information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The estimate is sold">
    Selling the estimate creates the **job** and the **Job Plan**, and fills in the **Crew Lead** and scheduling so the work can be carried out.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The crew does the work">
    The crew works from the Job Plan, captures **Media** on site, completes **Forms**, and keeps the schedule up to date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The job is billed and closed">
    Once the work is done, **Invoices** and **Financial Summary** track payment, and the job is closed out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
You manage the estimate's status (In Progress, Sold, and so on) on the estimate itself — the project simply reflects whatever is happening with it.
</Note>

## Who can do what

- **Sales roles and above** can open projects and edit their details and contacts.
- A few actions are more protected — for example, only **sales roles** (Admin, Sales Admin, Sales Member) can create or delete a project.

If a button or tab isn't available to you, it's because your role doesn't include that action — check with a manager if you think you need access.
