The **Clients** page is where every customer you've taken on lives. From here you can search the full list, open any client to see their full summary, and edit the details that follow them across jobs — their contact info, billing address, and the date they became a client.

<Note>
A client is created when you convert a lead into one. If your list is empty, start by converting a lead — the new client then appears here automatically.
</Note>

## Browse your clients

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Clients page">
    Go to **Clients** from the main navigation. The heading reads **Clients**, and the page lists everyone you work with.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search for someone">
    Use the search box at the top — **Search by name, email, phone or address…** — to narrow the list as you type. Any client whose details match shows up instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a client's summary">
    Click **View Summary** on a client's row to open their full profile.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The list shows each client's **Name**, **Email**, **Phone**, and **Last Activity** (the most recent time anything about them changed), so you can scan for the right person at a glance.


## The client summary

Opening a client brings you to their summary header, which gathers the essentials in one place:

- Their **name** and a **Client since** date.
- Quick-tap **email**, **phone**, and **address** links — tap the email to start a message, the phone to call, or the address to open it in maps.
- Their projects, which you can open to dig into individual jobs.

<Tip>
On a phone, contact details tuck behind a **View contact details** link to keep the screen tidy. Tap it to expand email, phone, and address.
</Tip>

## Edit client details

Use the edit option to update a client's contact people, billing address, and client-since date in one place.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the editor">
    On the client summary, click the **pencil** icon next to the client's name. The **Edit Client Details** window opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update contacts">
    Each contact appears as its own card (**Contact 1**, **Contact 2**, and so on). Update names, phone, and email as needed. To add another person, click **Add Contact**. See [Manage contacts](/guides/clients/contacts) for the full rundown on primary and secondary contacts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the billing address">
    Under **Billing Address**, start typing in the **Address** field and pick the match from the suggestions. This is the address used for billing, which can differ from where the work happens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the Client Since date">
    Use the **Client Since** date picker to record when the relationship began. This is what shows as “Client since” on the summary.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save changes**. Your updates apply right away. To back out without saving, click **Cancel**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Delete a client

If a client account was created by mistake or is no longer needed, you can remove it.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the menu">
    On the client summary, click the **three-dots** menu in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Delete Client">
    Select **Delete Client**. A confirmation window asks you to confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Delete** to remove the client. This can't be undone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Deleting a client is reserved for managers. If you don't see the three-dots menu or the **Delete Client** option, your role doesn't include it — ask a manager for help.
</Note>
