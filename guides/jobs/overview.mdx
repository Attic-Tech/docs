The **Jobs** page is your home base for every job at your branch. From here you can see each job's status at a glance, find the one you're looking for, and jump straight to whatever needs doing next — assigning a crew lead, reviewing a plan, or scheduling the work.

You'll find it as **Jobs** in the main navigation. The heading at the top reads **Jobs**, with a short reminder underneath: *View job info, status, and any action items.*

## Read the jobs list

By default the page shows your jobs in a table. Each row is one job, with these columns:

- **Client** — the client the job belongs to. Click the name to open the client's page, or a dash if no client is linked yet.
- **Project** — the project the job is part of. Click it to open the client's project page, where the job's details, plan, and forms live.
- **Assigned To** — the crew lead responsible for the job, or a dash if no one is assigned yet.
- **Created** — the date the job was created.
- **Updated At** — the last time anything on the job changed.
- **Status** — where the job is in its lifecycle (see below).
- **Work Area** — the areas of the property the job covers, shown as tags.
- **Actions** — a button that takes you to the next step for that job.

<Note>
The list shows the jobs for your current branch. Cancelled jobs are hidden by default — switch to the **Cancelled** tab (or add it as a status filter) to see them.
</Note>

## Understand job status

Every job carries a **Status** that tells you where it is in the process — from **Requires Crew Lead** when it's first created, through planning, scheduling, and production, to **Closed Job** or **Cancelled**. For what each status means and how a job moves between them, see [Job status lifecycle](/reference/jobs/job-status-lifecycle).

The tabs across the top of the list — starting with **All Jobs** — let you jump to a single status, and each tab shows a count so you can see how many jobs sit in each stage.

### Change a job's status

If you have permission, the **Status** cell is a dropdown. Open it on the job's row and pick a new status. A confirmation appears (**Status updated**) once the change saves.

<Note>
The dropdown lets you set the job to any status, so move it deliberately. If you don't have permission to change status, the status simply shows as a colored tag instead of a dropdown.
</Note>

## Find a specific job

There are a few ways to narrow the list down.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search by name">
    Use the search box at the top left — it reads **Type a name...** — and start typing. The list filters as you type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter by status or date">
    Open the filters to narrow by **Status** (pick one or more) or by **Date Created** (choose a date range). These stack with search, so you can combine them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the status tabs">
    For a quick cut, click a status tab at the top of the list (for example **Pending Review**) to see only the jobs in that stage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You can sort the table by clicking a column header — for example, sort by **Created** or **Updated At** to bring the newest or most recently touched jobs to the top.
</Tip>

## Switch between table and map

In the top right of the list there's a view toggle. Switch to the **map** view to see your jobs plotted by location instead of listed in a table. Click any pin to see a quick summary — project, phone, email, and branch — with a link to the job's project page. Switch back to the table view at any time; your search and filters carry over.

## Jump to the next action

The **Actions** column gives each job a single button for its next step, based on its status:

- **Assign Crew Lead** — for a job that still needs a crew lead.
- **View Profile** — opens the client's project page to see the job's details and progress.
- **Review Plan** — opens the plan so you can review and approve it.
- **Schedule Job** — takes you to the schedule to place the job.

Click the button to go straight to that task. On phones, each job appears as a card with the same status, details, and action button.

<Tip>
When a job has linked project media, you'll also see a small image icon next to the action button — click it to open that project's media.
</Tip>
