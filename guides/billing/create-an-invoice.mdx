---
title: "Create an invoice"
description: "Bill a customer for a project — start from a sold estimate or build line items from scratch, set your dates and terms, then save it as a draft or send it straight away."

An invoice is how you bill a customer for work on a project. You can build one in two ways: pull the line items straight from a sold estimate, or start fresh and add items yourself. Either way, you set the issue date and payment terms, choose what the customer sees, and finish by saving a draft or sending the invoice by email.

<Note>
You start every invoice by choosing a **client** and a **project** — an invoice always belongs to one project, so the work and the customer are clear.
</Note>

## Start a new invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Invoices page">
    Go to **Invoices** from the main navigation. This is where every invoice lives — drafts, sent, and paid. From here you can search, filter, and reopen any of them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Invoice">
    Press the **Create Invoice** button in the top-right corner. A short two-step setup opens.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the client and project">
    Pick the **Client** and then the **Project** the invoice is for. The project list fills in once a client is selected. Click **Continue**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You can also start an invoice from inside a project. When you do, the client and project are filled in for you, and you jump straight to choosing how to add items.
</Tip>

## Choose how to add items

On the second step you pick one of two starting points:

- **Import From Estimate** — pull the items from an estimate that's already been marked sold on this project. This is the fastest way to bill exactly what was quoted.
- **Create New** — start with an empty invoice and add items yourself, either from your catalogue or as custom one-off lines.

### Importing from a sold estimate

If you choose **Import From Estimate**, pick the estimate from the **Estimates** dropdown. Each option shows the date it was sold and its price, so you can tell them apart. If the project has only one sold estimate, it's selected for you automatically.

<Note>
A sold estimate is required to import. If the project has no sold estimates yet, you'll see a notice — switch to **Create New**, or go mark an estimate sold first.
</Note>

Click **Continue** to open the full invoice with those items already filled in.

## Fill in the invoice details

Whichever path you took, you now land on the invoice builder. The top section, **Invoice Details**, shows the **Client Name** and **Project** you chose, plus three things you set:

- **Issue Date** — the date the invoice is dated. Defaults to today.
- **Payment Terms** — choose from the terms your workspace has set up. The one marked as default is picked automatically.
- **Due Date** — calculated for you from the issue date and the terms you pick, so you don't have to work it out.

There's also a **Description of work** box where you can write a short summary of what was done. This appears on the invoice the customer sees.

<Note>
If a payment term is marked **(disabled)** in the list, it's no longer in normal use — pick an active term instead.
</Note>

## Review and adjust the line items

The **Line Items** card shows everything the customer will be billed for, with a running breakdown of subtotal, any tax, and the total.

### When you imported from an estimate

The items come straight from the estimate and are shown as a clean list. To change the actual items or quantities, you update the estimate itself — the **Edit Line Items** note explains this.

You can still adjust two things here:

- **Customize the amount due.** If you only want to bill part of the job right now (a deposit, say), use the customize option on the breakdown. You can enter a flat dollar amount or a percentage of the project total, and the invoice scales to match. Use the reset option to go back to the full amount.
- **Client View Settings.** Two switches — **Unit Price** and **Total Price** — control whether those columns show on the customer's copy. Turn them off for a simpler, total-only invoice.

The tax is carried over from the estimate and shown as a **read-only** line in the breakdown, so the invoice can't drift from what the customer agreed to. To change the tax, update the estimate itself.

### When you started from scratch

Click **Add New Item** to add a line. For each line you can:

- Search your catalogue and pick an item, or type a name and choose **Add as custom item** to create a one-off line.
- Set the **Qty/Unit**, the **Unit Price**, or just the **Total Price** — the others adjust to match.
- Remove a line with the trash icon (you'll be asked to confirm).

The breakdown at the bottom keeps the totals up to date as you go.

You also set the tax here. The **Tax** row in the breakdown has a selector, and your branch's default rate (if it has one) is applied automatically. Open the selector to switch to any other saved rate (the default one is marked **(Default)**), choose **No Tax**, or use **Create new** to add a rate on the spot. The invoice total updates as soon as you change the selection.

## Add a disclaimer

The **Disclaimer** card holds any terms or notes you want printed on the invoice — warranty language, payment instructions, anything standard for your work. It comes pre-filled with your workspace's default text, and you can edit it for this one invoice.

## Save or send

When the invoice looks right, use the buttons at the bottom of the screen:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Save as Draft">
    Choose **Save as Draft** to store the invoice without sending it. It lands in your Invoices list as a draft, where you can reopen, finish, and send it later.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and Send">
    Choose **Save and Send** to email the invoice now. This opens a short email window where you confirm the **Recipients**, the **Subject**, and the **Body** — all pre-filled for you, including the customer's email and a friendly default message. Add or remove recipients as needed, then send.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**Save and Send** needs at least one line item — you can't send an empty invoice. **Save as Draft** is always available so you can come back to it.
</Note>

<Tip>
After sending, you land on the invoice's detail page. From there you can record payments, resend the email, download the PDF, or void the invoice if something's wrong.
</Tip>
