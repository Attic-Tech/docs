---
title: "Void an invoice"
description: "Cancel a sent invoice you no longer want collected — record a reason, add an internal note, and mark it Voided so the client no longer owes payment."
icon: "ban"
---

Voiding an invoice cancels it for good. Once an invoice has been sent and is sitting in **Open** status — meaning the client owes a balance — voiding it clears that outstanding amount, sets the invoice's status to **Voided**, and stops it from being collected. Use it when an invoice was sent in error, the work was cancelled, or it's being replaced by a corrected one.

<Note>
Voiding is not the same as deleting. A **Draft** invoice that was never sent can simply be deleted. Voiding is for an invoice that has already gone out, so a record of it stays in your history.
</Note>

## Before you start

You can only void an invoice that's in **Open** status — sent, with a balance still owed.

- A **Draft** invoice can't be voided. Delete it instead, or send it first.
- A **Paid** or **Partially Paid** invoice can't be voided. If money has already been recorded against it, you'll work with a credit or payment instead.
- An invoice that's already **Voided** stays that way — the action can't be reversed.

<Tip>
Not sure of an invoice's status? Open the **Invoices** page and use the status tabs across the top (**All**, **Draft**, **Open**, **Partially Paid**, **Paid**, **Voided**) to see where each one stands.
</Tip>

## Void the invoice

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invoice">
    Go to the **Invoices** page and click the invoice you want to cancel. This opens its detail view, where you can see its line items, status, and history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the actions menu">
    Click the **More options** button — the three-dot icon in the top-right of the invoice. From the menu, choose **Void Invoice**. (You'll only see this option when the invoice is in **Open** status.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the warning">
    A **Void Invoice** window opens with a warning banner. It confirms that voiding clears any outstanding amount due, that the client will no longer owe payment, and that the status will change to **Voided**. This action cannot be undone, so make sure you have the right invoice.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a reason">
    Choose a **Reason** from the dropdown. This explains why the invoice is being cancelled and is required. (The choices in this list are set up by your workspace — see below.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an internal note">
    Type an **Internal Note** describing the situation. This is required and is for your team's records only — it isn't shown to the client.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Void Invoice** to finish. You'll see a confirmation that the invoice has been voided, and its status updates to **Voided**. To back out without changing anything, click **Cancel** instead.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens after voiding

- The invoice's status becomes **Voided**, and it shows up under the **Voided** tab on the Invoices page.
- Any outstanding balance is cleared, so the client no longer owes payment on it.
- The invoice stays in your records — it isn't removed — so you keep a complete history of what was billed and why it was cancelled.

<Note>
Voiding can't be undone. If you voided the wrong invoice, you'll need to create a new invoice to bill the work again.
</Note>

## Manage the list of void reasons

The reasons you can pick when voiding are defined per branch by your workspace. To review or add to them, go to **Settings**, open **Invoices**, and select the **Credit & Void Reasons** tab.

There you can add a new reason with **Create Reason** — give it a **Name**, choose its **Reason Type**, add an optional **Description**, set whether it's **Enabled**, and set a **Sort Order** that controls where it appears in the dropdown. Disabling a reason hides it from the void window without removing past records that used it.

<Tip>
Keep your reason list short and clear — for example "Billed in error", "Job cancelled", or "Replaced by corrected invoice". Clean reasons make your voided-invoice history easy to scan later.
</Tip>
