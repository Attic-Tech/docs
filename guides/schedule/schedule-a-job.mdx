---
title: "Schedule a job"
description: "Put a job on the calendar step by step — pick the client and job, set the day, time, and crew, then save it as a draft or publish it to your team."
icon: "calendar-plus"
---

Scheduling a job is how you tell your team who is working, where, and when. You start from the **Schedule** page, choose the job, fill in one or more days of work, assign the crew, and then either save your progress as a draft or publish it so everyone gets notified.

<Note>
You can build the schedule a little at a time. Save it as a draft whenever you want and come back later — nothing is sent to your team until you publish.
</Note>

## Open the New Job form

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Schedule page">
    Open **Schedule** from the main navigation. This is the calendar where every job, task, and time-off entry for your branch lives.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create New, then Job">
    Press the **Create New** button in the top-right corner and choose **Job** from the menu. A panel titled **Schedule New Job** slides in from the right.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Already looking at the right day on the calendar? When you add a job, the form starts on that date — so navigate to the week you have in mind before you open it.
</Tip>

## Pick the client and job

At the top of the panel you'll choose what you're scheduling.

- **Client** — Start typing a name in the **Search client** box and pick the customer from the list.
- **Job** — Choose the **Job** for that client. If the client has only one open job, it fills in automatically. Otherwise, search and select the one you want.
- **Address** — This fills in on its own from the job and is shown for reference.

<Note>
You have to pick a **Job** before you can save or publish. The job is what ties the schedule to the right customer, address, and crew.
</Note>

## Fill in the day's details

Each day of work is its own tab. You start on **Day 1**. For the selected day, fill in:

- **Shift Title** — An optional label for the day, for example *Day 1 - Smith Residence*. If you leave it blank, the day is simply called "Day 1," "Day 2," and so on.
- **Team Lead** — The crew leader running the day. Pick from the list of available leads.
- **Job Date** — Click the date button and choose the day on the calendar.
- **Start Time** and **End Time** — Set when the shift begins and ends.
- **Shift Notes** — Optional notes for anything the crew should know about that day.

## Assign the crew

Use the **Assign To** field to choose who works the shift.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the team picker">
    Click the **Assign To** box. You can browse people by role — **Crew Member**, **Crew Leader**, or **Subcontractor** — or just start typing a name in the **Type to search** box.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your people">
    Check off everyone working that day. Each person is grouped under **Available** or **Unavailable** for the date and time you set, so you can see at a glance who has a conflict before you assign them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a subcontractor if needed">
    Under the **Subcontractor** list, choose **+ Add subcontractor** to create one on the spot. Enter their name (and an optional email and phone for notifications), then **Add**. They're selected for the day right away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
A person marked **Unavailable** already has a shift or time off that overlaps this one. You can still assign them, but it's worth checking the [Resolve a conflict](/guides/schedule/resolve-a-conflict) guide first.
</Tip>

## Add more days

Many jobs run across several days. To add another:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + Add Day">
    Press **+ Add Day** above the day's details. A new tab appears (**Day 2**, **Day 3**, and so on), pre-set to the next date.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the new day">
    Switch between days using the tabs and fill in each one's time and crew, just like the first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove a day you don't need">
    Select the day's tab and click the trash icon next to it to remove it. Removing a day that was already saved asks you to confirm first.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Save a draft or publish

The buttons at the bottom of the panel decide what happens next.

### Save as a draft

Click **Save Draft** to keep your work without notifying anyone. The day shows up on the calendar as a draft so you (and others who can see the schedule) know it's a work in progress. Come back any time to finish it.

### Publish the job

When the schedule is ready, click **Publish Job** and choose what to send:

- **All Shifts** — publish every day at once.
- **Publish Day 1**, **Publish Day 2**, … — publish a single day and leave the rest as drafts.

<Note>
A day can only be published once it's complete — it needs a date, a start and end time, and at least one person assigned. Until then, that day's publish option stays greyed out.
</Note>

### Choose who gets notified

When you publish, a **Customize Notifications** window appears so you control who hears about it:

- **Notify Assigned Team** — email the people assigned to the shift.
- **Notify Sales Person** — email the salesperson on the job.
- **Email Client** — email the customer. Their primary email is filled in for you, and you can add more recipients with the **Email** button.

Turn each one on or off, then confirm to publish.

<Tip>
Saving a draft can still email the client if you want a heads-up sent, but it never notifies your team — only publishing does that.
</Tip>

## Edit or delete a scheduled job

To change a job you've already scheduled, open it again from the calendar. The same panel reopens with its days filled in, so you can adjust times, swap crew, add days, or republish.

To remove it entirely, open the job and click **Delete**. You'll be asked whether to notify the assigned team and the client about the cancellation before it's removed.
