The **win feed** is your team's channel for celebrating good work. It's a running feed of posts — a closed sale, hours saved on a job, a badge someone unlocked, or just a shout-out — that everyone in the channel can see, react to, and comment on. Each channel has its own banner (the **Wall of Wins**), its own members, and, when it's set up for it, a **leaderboard** down the side.

This guide covers reading and using a win feed day to day. Posting an AI-drafted celebration from a real win, reacting and commenting, and reading the leaderboard each have their own guide — see [What's next](#whats-next).

<Note>
Win channels are a feature your workspace turns on. If you don't see any channels in your navigation, they aren't enabled for your team yet.
</Note>

## Open a win feed

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find your channels">
    Look for your win channels in the side navigation. Each channel is one feed — your team may have one or several.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the channel">
    Click a channel to open its feed. The channel name appears at the top as a heading (for example, `#wall-of-wins`), with the channel's description just beneath it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Read the feed

Posts appear newest-first under the channel header. Each post shows who posted it, when, and the message — which may include a photo, a GIF, or a celebrated win. As you scroll, older posts load automatically.

- **No posts yet?** A new channel shows **No posts yet** with a friendly nudge to *Share something with your team!* — be the first to post.
- **Replies** sit under a post in a thread. If a post has replies, you can collapse or expand the thread to keep the feed tidy.
- **React** to any post or comment with an emoji, and **add your own comment** in the box under each post.

<Tip>
Reacting is the fastest way to show support — one tap and your teammate sees the love. See the [React and comment](/guides/earnings/comment-react) guide for the full set of reactions.
</Tip>

## Post to the feed

The post box sits at the top of the feed, right under the banner. It opens with a friendly prompt: *🎉 Share a win, shout out a teammate....*

<Steps>
  <Step title="Write your message">
    Click the box and type whatever you want to share.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a GIF or media (optional)">
    Use **Gif** to search and drop in a GIF, or **Media** to attach a photo or file. On a phone, tap the **+** button to reach the same **Select Gif** and **Upload Media** options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post it">
    Press **Post**. Your message appears at the top of the feed for everyone in the channel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The **Post** button stays off until you've added something to share. Once your message (or attachment) is ready, it turns on.
</Note>

### Celebrate a real win with AI

Next to the post box you'll see **Use AI**. This opens a short, guided **Create Win Post** flow that turns a real, logged win — a **New Sale**, **Job Hours Saved**, or a **Badge Unlocked** — into a ready-to-share, written-up celebration. You pick the win, add an optional note, and review the draft before it posts.

For the full walkthrough, see [Create an AI win post](/guides/earnings/create-ai-win-post).

<Tip>
In the win picker you can filter by **All Wins**, **New Sale**, **Job Hours Saved**, or **Badge Unlocked** to find the moment you want to celebrate.
</Tip>

## Check the leaderboard

When a channel has a leaderboard, it appears in a panel beside the feed on a wide screen. On a phone, tap **View Leaderboard** above the feed to open it.

If the channel tracks more than one kind of standing, use the dropdown at the top of the panel to switch between them. Your own row is highlighted so you can spot where you stand at a glance.

For how rankings are figured and what each standing means, see the [Leaderboard](/guides/earnings/leaderboard) guide.

## Set the channel banner

If you manage channels, you can give a feed its own banner — the image across the top of the **Wall of Wins**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hover the banner area">
    At the top of the feed, hover over the banner (or the empty banner placeholder on a fresh channel).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload an image">
    Click **Upload New** and choose an image. It saves and refreshes in place, and you'll see a **Banner updated** confirmation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Swap or remove it later">
    Hover the banner again to **Upload New** (replace it) or **Remove Image** to clear it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Managing the banner and channel members is limited to people who manage channels. If you don't see these controls, you have the standard member view — you can still post, react, and comment.
</Note>
