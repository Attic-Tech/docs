Your **Performance dashboard** is the home screen you land on when you sign in. It rolls up your numbers — and your team's — into a single, scannable page: a friendly greeting, an **Overview** of headline metrics, and a **Leaderboard** that shows where you stand.

What you see depends on your role and on which views are turned on for your branch. This guide walks through every control on the page so you can read it with confidence.

<Note>
You don't change any numbers here. The dashboard reflects work that's already been recorded — completed jobs, sold estimates, customer reviews, and bonuses. Your job is to read it and use it.
</Note>

## Open the dashboard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    The dashboard is your home page, so you'll usually see it as soon as you sign in. If you've navigated elsewhere, choose **Home** from the main navigation to come back.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the greeting">
    The top of the page greets you by name — **Hi, &lt;your name&gt;**. Just below it sits everything else: the Overview and the Leaderboard.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
If the dashboard isn't available for your role, you'll be sent to whichever page you do have access to (for example, Leads, Clients, Schedule, Jobs, the Calculator, your Wallet, or Shifts). That's expected — it just means your work starts somewhere other than this screen.
</Tip>

## Pick your view: Operations or Sales

If both views are turned on for you, a small dropdown in the top-right corner lets you switch between them:

- **Operations** — your hands-on production numbers: jobs completed, hours saved, customer reviews, and bonus payout.
- **Sales** — your selling numbers: pipeline, closing ratio, average sale, and revenue.

Pick the one that matches what you want to look at. If only one view is available to you, the dropdown doesn't appear and the page simply shows that view.

<Note>
The view you choose also changes the **Leaderboard** lower on the page, so an Operations view shows production rankings and a Sales view shows selling rankings.
</Note>

## The Overview

The **Overview** section is the band of metric cards near the top, with a chart on the left and summary cards on the right. Each card shows a headline number with a small **up or down badge** comparing it to the previous period — green for an increase, red for a decrease.

<Tip>
Click any metric card to load that metric into the chart. It's an easy way to see the trend behind a single number over time.
</Tip>

### Choose what you're looking at

A few controls sit just above the cards:

- **Person picker** — if your role lets you view other people, this dropdown lets you choose whose numbers to show. It also offers a **branch-wide** option so you can see the whole branch's totals instead of one person's.
- **Branch / Company** — this toggle decides whether the numbers (and rankings) cover just your branch or the whole company. It appears when you're viewing an individual's figures.
- **Period** (Operations view) — switch the time window with the **Weekly / Monthly / Quarterly** toggle.
- **Date range** (Sales view) — instead of a period toggle, the Sales view gives you a **date-range picker** so you can set an exact start and end date.

### Operations cards

In the Operations view you'll see these cards:

- **Jobs Completed** — how many jobs were finished in the period.
- **Total Hours Saved** — the labor hours saved versus what was estimated.
- **Bonus Payout** — the bonus earned for the period, in dollars.
- **My Reviews** (or **Branch Reviews** when you're viewing the whole branch) — the average customer star rating out of 5, the number of reviews in parentheses, and a **review rate** badge showing what share of jobs got a review.

### Sales cards

In the Sales view you'll see these cards:

- **Pending Pipeline** — the dollar value of opportunities still in play.
- **Closing Ratio** — the percentage of opportunities won, with a badge showing the **sold / counted** opportunity counts (it reads **N/A** when there were no opportunities to count).
- **Average Sale** — the average dollar value of a sold estimate.
- **Total Sold** — the total dollar value of everything sold in the period.
- **Total Paid** — the revenue that's been closed and paid.

Below the Sales cards is an **Include subcontractor sales** switch. Turn it on to fold subcontractor work into the totals, or off to leave it out.

## The Leaderboard

Scroll down to the **Leaderboard** to see how people rank against one another for the selected period and scope. It has two parts:

- A **podium** highlighting the top performers.
- A **table** listing everyone, with your own row marked as **You** and pinned so it's easy to find.

The table's columns change with your view.

### Operations rankings

The Operations leaderboard ranks on these columns:

- **AVG PRODUCTION** — the value of all jobs divided by total job hours.
- **AVG SAVINGS** — total hours saved as a percentage of estimated labor hours.
- **HOURLY KICK UP** — total bonus divided by total hours worked.

### Sales rankings

The Sales leaderboard ranks on these columns:

- **CLOSING RATIO** — sold opportunities divided by counted opportunities.
- **AVERAGE SALE** — total revenue divided by the number of sold estimates.
- **TOTAL SALES** — the sum of all sold estimate values.
- **REVENUE PER LEAD** — total revenue divided by total leads.

<Note>
When you set the scope to **Company**, the table adds a **BRANCH** column so you can see which branch each person belongs to. Each person's number is added up across all the branches they work in.
</Note>

<Tip>
Hover over a column heading to see exactly how that metric is calculated. On your own row, the leaderboard also shows how much more you'd need to climb to pass the person ranked just above you.
</Tip>
