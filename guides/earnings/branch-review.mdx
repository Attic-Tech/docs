Branch reviews are the customer testimonials that appear on the proposals you send out. Each one shows a star rating, what the customer said, and who said it. They live with your **Proposals** settings and are managed one branch at a time, so every branch can show off its own happy customers.

<Note>
Branch reviews are managed in Workspace Settings, so you'll need an account that can manage settings for your workspace (typically an admin or manager).
</Note>

## Where branch reviews show up

When a proposal goes out, it includes a short **"Join thousands of happy customers"** section with up to two reviews for that branch. Each review displays:

- The **star rating** (1 to 5 stars)
- The **review text** — what the customer said about the work
- The **author** and their **location** (for example, *Jane Doe, Austin, TX*)

Keeping a few strong reviews on file gives every proposal instant credibility.

## Open the branch reviews list

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Proposals settings">
    From **Settings**, open **Proposals**. The page is titled **Proposals** with the note "Tailor your proposal settings for each branch."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Customer Reviews tab">
    Along the top you'll see the proposal tabs — **Proposal Content**, **Financing Terms**, **Customer Reviews**, and **Payment Methods**. Click **Customer Reviews**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the branch">
    If your workspace has more than one branch, use the **branch selector** in the top-right to choose which branch you're editing. Reviews are saved per branch, so make sure you're on the right one before you add or change anything.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
The branch you're working on carries over as you switch between the proposal tabs, so you can set up content, financing, reviews, and payment methods for one branch in a single pass.
</Tip>

## Add a review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Create New">
    On the **Customer Reviews** tab, click **Create New** to open the **Create Customer Review** form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the star rating">
    In **Stars**, enter a rating from 1 to 5. This controls how many filled stars appear on the proposal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the author">
    In **Author**, type the name the review should appear under — for example, *Jane Doe*. This is the name customers will see.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the location">
    In **Location**, add where the customer is — for example, *Austin, TX*. It shows next to the author's name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write the review text">
    In **Description**, paste or type what the customer said about your work. This is the main body of the review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save it">
    Click **Create**. The review is added to the list and becomes available to show on that branch's proposals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
All four fields — stars, author, location, and description — are required, and the **Create** button stays disabled until they're all filled in correctly. There are length limits on the author, location, and review text; if you go over, you'll be asked to shorten it.
</Note>

## Find and search reviews

The **Customer Reviews** tab lists every review for the selected branch, with columns for **Author**, **Stars**, **Description**, and **Location**. To narrow things down:

- Use the **Search by name** box at the top to find a review by its author.
- Click the **Author**, **Stars**, or **Location** column headers to sort the list.

<Tip>
If the list says "No customer reviews found," either this branch has no reviews yet or your search didn't match anything. Clear the search box or click **Create New** to add your first one.
</Tip>

## Edit a review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the review">
    Find the review in the list and click the **pencil (edit)** icon on its row. On a phone, tap the **three-dots** menu and choose **Edit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make your changes">
    The **Edit Customer Review** form opens with the current stars, author, location, and text already filled in. Update whatever you need.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your changes">
    Click **Save Changes**. The button only enables once you've actually changed something, so you can't save an unedited review by accident.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Remove a review

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start the delete">
    On the review's row, click the **trash (delete)** icon. On a phone, tap the **three-dots** menu and choose **Delete**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    A confirmation appears: the review from that author "will be removed and will no longer appear on new proposals." Click **Delete** to confirm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Deleting a review can't be undone, and it only affects **new** proposals going forward — proposals you've already sent keep the reviews they were created with.
</Note>

## Tips for great branch reviews

<Tip>
Only the first couple of reviews show on a proposal, so lead with your strongest ones. Short, specific, five-star reviews that name a real result tend to land best.
</Tip>

- Keep each branch's reviews local to that branch's customers — it feels more genuine to the people reading the proposal.
- Refresh them now and then so your proposals always reflect recent, relevant work.
- Double-check the author and location spelling before saving; that small detail is what makes a review feel real.
