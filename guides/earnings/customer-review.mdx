A **Customer Review** is the star rating a customer gives for a finished job, plus an optional link to the public review they left online. You record it right on the job, and it stays attached to that customer and project. Capturing reviews is how great work gets noticed — a strong rating becomes part of your team's recognition and shows the crew their work paid off.

<Note>
You don't write the review yourself — you record what the customer said. The rating is from 0 to 5 stars, and the link points to the customer's public review (for example, the one they left on Google).
</Note>

## Where to find it

There are two places to add or update a customer review. Both save the same rating to the same job, so you can use whichever is in front of you.

- **On the job.** Open the job and look in the **Job Details** section for **Customer Review**.
- **On the project header.** When you're viewing a customer's project, the review controls sit in the top row next to the tabs — a row of stars with an **Add Review** (or **Edit**) link.

<Note>
If you can see the rating but can't change it, your role has view-only access to customer reviews. You'll still see the stars and, if one was added, the review link.
</Note>

## Record a review on the job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the job and find Customer Review">
    Go to the job, open the **Job Details** section, and find the **Customer Review** panel. Before a rating is set it reads **Click to rate this review**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the star rating">
    Click a star to set the rating from 1 to 5. The panel updates to **Rating received**. To clear a rating you set by mistake, click the same star again to set it back to empty.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the review link (optional)">
    Once you've set a rating, a **Google Review Link** field appears. Paste the link to the customer's public review into the **Paste Google review URL...** box and press **Enter** (or click out of the field) to save it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
You don't need a separate save button on the job. The rating saves the moment you click a star, and the link saves when you finish typing and leave the field. A small **Saved** note confirms each change went through.
</Tip>

A valid link is the full web address of the customer's review. If you paste something that isn't a usable link, the field flags it and keeps your text so you can fix it — nothing is lost. To remove a link you added, clear the field (use the small remove button next to it) and it saves as empty.

## Record a review from the project header

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the customer's project">
    From the customer's project, look at the row along the top, next to the tabs. You'll see a row of stars and a link that reads **Add Review** (or **Edit** if a rating is already there).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the rating">
    Click a star to set the rating. A small panel opens so you can add the review link in the same place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the link and save">
    Paste the review link into the **Review URL** box and click **Save**. While it's saving the button reads **Saving...**, and a check mark appears when it's done. Close the panel with the **X** when you're finished.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Both spots share the same rating. A review you add on the job shows up on the project header, and the other way around — there's only ever one rating per job.
</Note>

## Tips for clean reviews

<Tip>
Set the rating as soon as you hear it from the customer, while the details are fresh. You can always update it later if the customer revises their feedback.
</Tip>

- **Only record real customer feedback.** The rating should reflect what the customer actually said, not your own opinion of the job.
- **Always include the link when there is one.** A public review link lets others confirm the rating and see the customer's own words.
- **Clearing the rating clears the panel.** If you set the rating back to empty, the link field is cleared too — set a rating first, then add the link.

## What happens next

A recorded customer review travels with the job and the customer's project, so anyone who opens them later can see how the job landed. Strong reviews are part of how good work gets surfaced and celebrated across your team's recognition — the better your crew does for customers, the more it shows.
