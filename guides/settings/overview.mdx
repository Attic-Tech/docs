**Settings** is where you configure how your workspace behaves — your company details, your branches, your team, your pricing, and the way estimates, proposals, and invoices look and work. Think of it as the control panel behind the day-to-day app: change something here once, and it applies everywhere your team works.

<Note>
Most of Settings is **Admin-only**. If you don't see a section described below, your account doesn't have access to it — that's expected, and an administrator on your team can make changes for you.
</Note>

## Open Settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Settings">
    Open **Settings** from the main navigation. The first available section opens automatically, so you'll land on a real page rather than a blank screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the sidebar to move around">
    The left sidebar lists every section you can open, grouped into **General**, **Sales & Operations**, and **System Configuration**. Click any item to jump to it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Search to jump straight to a setting">
    At the top of the sidebar is a **search box** ("Type here to search..."). Start typing — for example *deposit*, *timezone*, or *payment terms* — and it suggests the matching section or field so you can go right to it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
On a phone or tablet, the sidebar tucks into a menu at the top of the page. Tap it to see the same sections and search.
</Tip>

## What's in each section

The sidebar groups everything into three areas.

### General

The basics about your business, your locations, and your people.

- **Organization** — your company's name, logo, timezone, and core details. This is the foundation everything else builds on. See [Organization](/guides/settings/organization).
- **Branch Configuration** — each branch (location) and the defaults that drive its pricing and costs: labor rates, deposit percentages, discounts, fees, and more. See [Branch configuration](/guides/settings/branches).
- **Users** — the people on your team, the roles they have, and which branches they belong to. See [Users](/guides/settings/users) and [Roles and branches](/guides/settings/roles-branches).
- **Subcontractors** — the outside crews you work with, kept in one place. See [Subcontractors](/guides/settings/subcontractors).

### Sales & Operations

Everything that shapes how you quote, schedule, and bill work.

- **Leads** — how new leads are categorized as they move through your pipeline. See [Lead statuses](/guides/settings/statuses).
- **Price Book** — the heart of your estimating: the items you sell, the work areas you estimate, and the factors and costs that adjust the price. See the price-book guides below.
- **Proposals** — the look and content of the customer-facing proposals you send, plus the payment and financing options shown on them. See [Proposal content](/guides/settings/proposal-content) and [Payment methods and financing](/guides/settings/payment-methods-financing).
- **Calendar** — how the schedule behaves and the types of tasks your team can book. See [Task types](/guides/settings/task-types).
- **Invoices** — payment terms, the content that appears on invoices, and the reasons your team can pick when crediting or voiding. See [Payment terms](/guides/settings/payment-terms), [Invoice content and automations](/guides/settings/invoice-content-automations), and [Credit and void reasons](/guides/settings/credit-void-reasons).
- **Resources** — the equipment and vehicles your branches use. See [Equipment and vehicles](/guides/settings/equipment-vehicles).

### System Configuration

The shared labels and structures that keep your data organized.

- **Tags** — the labels you attach to leads and projects to group and find them. See [Tags](/guides/settings/tags).
- **Expenses** — the expense types your team can pick from when recording costs.
- **Media Folders** — the folders that keep your photos and documents organized.

## The Price Book in detail

The **Price Book** is large enough to have its own set of guides. It's where your estimates get their numbers, so it's worth setting up carefully.

- [Items](/guides/settings/price-book/items) — the products and services you sell, with their pricing.
- [Work areas](/guides/settings/price-book/work-areas) — the areas of a job you estimate, like an attic or a crawl space.
- [Factors and additional costs](/guides/settings/price-book/factors-additional-costs) — the conditions and extra charges that adjust a price up or down.
- [Multiplier ranges](/guides/settings/price-book/multiplier-ranges) — the price bands that scale costs across different job sizes.

<Tip>
If estimates are coming out too high or too low, the Price Book and your **Branch Configuration** defaults are the two places to check first — together they decide every price.
</Tip>

## A few things to keep in mind

<Note>
Settings changes apply across your workspace, so a change here can affect everyone's estimates, proposals, and invoices going forward. When you're adjusting pricing defaults, change one thing at a time and check the result on a test estimate before rolling it out widely.
</Note>

- **Branch defaults matter.** Many pricing numbers live on each branch, not on the price book item. If a price looks off for one location but right for another, compare their branch settings.
- **Roles decide who can do what.** If a teammate can't reach a section, it's their role — adjust it under **Users**.
- **Search is the fast path.** When you know the name of a setting but not where it lives, type it into the sidebar search instead of hunting through sections.
