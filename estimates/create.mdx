The **Calculator** is the guided, step-by-step wizard you use to build an estimate for a job. You tell it who the customer is, what work areas are involved, and what items and services go into each one. As you go, it calculates the price for you and turns everything into a clean breakdown you can save, print, and (if proposals are enabled for your branch) send to the customer.

<Note>
You don't need to know any pricing math. The Calculator handles the numbers — your job is to describe the work accurately.
</Note>

## Start a new estimate

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Estimates page">
    Go to **Estimates** from the main navigation. This is your home base — every estimate you've started or finished lives here, and you can search, filter, and reopen any of them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click New Estimate">
    Press the **New Estimate** button in the top-right corner. This opens the Calculator wizard on a blank estimate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
To pick up where you left off, just open the Estimates list and click the estimate you want. It reopens in the Calculator exactly where it left off.
</Tip>

## Walk through the wizard

The Calculator moves through a few steps. The buttons at the bottom of the screen — **Back** and **Continue** — carry you forward and back, and the final step's button becomes **Save Estimate**.

### Step 1 — Client details and work area

The first screen has two parts.

**Client Details.** Choose the **Client Name** and the **project** this estimate belongs to. You'll fill in the rest of the customer and address details a little later.

**Choose a work area.** Pick the area of the job you're estimating (for example, an attic or a crawl space). You can search for one if your branch has a long list.

After you pick a work area, you can:

- **Rename it** with an optional custom name, so a breakdown with several similar areas stays easy to read.
- **Answer the work area factors** — simple **Yes / No** questions about the conditions in that area. These help the estimate reflect the real job.


### Step 2 — Add items

Now you fill the work area with the actual items and services.

- The **category list** on the side lets you jump between groups of items.
- The **search box** finds an item by name.
- Set the **quantity** for each item you want to include.

Need more than one area on the same estimate? Click **Add Work Area** and the wizard loops you back to pick another one and add its items. Repeat for as many areas as the job needs.

<Tip>
The **Continue** button stays disabled until the current work area has at least one item — that's a gentle nudge that the area still needs something added.
</Tip>

### Step 3 — Job and inspection details (when enabled)

If your branch uses inspection reports, the wizard gives you a step to capture the fuller picture of the visit — job and customer details, property conditions, notes about what you saw, and anything the crew should know. Fill in what's relevant and continue. (If this step isn't part of your branch's flow, the wizard simply skips it.)

### Step 4 — Review the breakdown

The last step is the **Overall Job Breakdown**. This is the complete, itemized summary of the estimate, including the calculated total price.

Here you can:

- **Review the price** and the line-by-line breakdown.
- **Add another work area** if you realize something's missing.
- **Print** the breakdown for your records or to hand to the customer.
- Add **Crew Notes** with anything the team should know about the job.

## Helpful features

### Build the estimate with AI

When the AI Calculator is turned on for your branch, a brand-new estimate offers to build itself for you. On the first step you'll see a **Use AI instead** option (and you can always switch back with **Start manually**).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Describe the job">
    Type a plain-language description like *"2500 sqft attic, blow in R-38 cellulose"* and send it. The AI reads it and populates the work areas and items for you. You can also tap the microphone to **speak** the description instead of typing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Or use photos">
    Switch to the **Photos** tab, add up to 10 property photos (upload or snap one on your phone), add any extra context, and choose **Analyze Photos and Generate Estimate**. The AI turns what it sees into a starting estimate.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and continue">
    Once the AI fills things in, choose **Accept & Review Breakdown** (or **Skip to Breakdown**) to jump to the summary. Always look it over — the AI gives you a head start, not the final word.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The AI builder needs a **client and project selected** before it can generate anything, so set those at the top of the first step.
</Note>

### Refine with the chat assistant

While you're adding items or reviewing the breakdown, a small **sparkle button** sits in the bottom corner of the screen. Open it to ask the assistant to tweak the estimate in plain language — *"add a vapor barrier"* or *"what should I add?"* — and it updates the estimate for you.

### Let AI check for issues

In the breakdown, look for **AI: Check for issues**. It reviews the estimate and flags anything that looks missing or off — for example, an item it would expect given the work you described. Each note tells you what to look at, and you can dismiss any you've considered. A green **Estimate looks good** means nothing stood out. These are suggestions to help you catch gaps, not blockers.

## Finish: save and sell

### Save the estimate

On the breakdown step, the main button becomes **Save Estimate**. Click it to save your work. The estimate now lives in your **Estimates** list, where you can reopen, print, or update it any time.

<Note>
If you reopen and edit an estimate you saved earlier, you may be asked how to handle pricing when you save again:

- **Keep original pricing and settings** — your changes to items, quantities, and details apply, but prices and labels stay exactly as they were.
- **Use up to date information** — pull in the latest prices, labels, and settings.

When in doubt, **Keep original** is the safe choice.
</Note>

### Turn it into a proposal

If proposals are enabled for your branch, you'll see **Generate Proposal** on the breakdown step. This creates the polished, customer-facing version of the estimate that you can share. Once created, the button becomes **View Proposal** so you can open it again.

### Mark it sold

When the customer accepts, you mark the estimate as **Sold** from the **Estimates** list. "Sold" simply means the customer said yes — it's how you and your team track which estimates turned into real, won work. If you ever need to correct when the deal actually closed, you can adjust the sold date on a sold estimate.

<Tip>
There's a limit on how many estimates you can keep open at once. If you hit it, the Calculator will let you know — wrap up a few open estimates (mark them sold or set them aside) to free up room for new ones.
</Tip>
