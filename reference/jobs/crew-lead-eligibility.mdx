This reference explains who can be picked as the **Team Lead** (the job's crew lead) when scheduling a job, why someone might not appear in the picker, and which roles are allowed to make the assignment.

The user-facing roles referenced below are: **Admin**, **Ops Manager**, **Sales Admin**, **Sales Member**, **Client Coordinator**, **Crew Leader**, and **Crew Member**.

## What the crew lead is

### The crew lead is the Team Lead on a scheduled day

When you schedule a job, each working day has a **Team Lead** field — this person is the job's crew lead for that day. A multi-day job can have a different Team Lead on each day.

The Team Lead is chosen per day in the New Job scheduling flow. It is not a separate record; it lives on the day you schedule.

### The crew lead is optional

A scheduled day can have no Team Lead. The field can be left empty, and an already-chosen Team Lead can be cleared back to empty.

## Who can be picked as the Team Lead

The Team Lead picker only offers people who meet all of the rules below. If someone is missing from the dropdown, one of these is the reason.

### Must hold the Crew Leader role

The Team Lead dropdown is limited to people who have the **Crew Leader** role. Someone without that role will not appear as an option, even if they are otherwise on the team.

### Must belong to the job's branch

Only people assigned to the job's branch are offered. People from other branches do not appear in the Team Lead picker. See [Org and branch scoping](/reference/platform/org-branch-scoping) for how branch membership controls who is visible.

### Must be an active, real team member

The picker hides:

- **Blocked members** — anyone whose account is blocked.
- **Reserved/system names** — internal placeholder entries (for example, names used for warranty calls, complaints, quality-control visits, or delivery costs) are never offered as a Team Lead.

### Two people stay selectable even if they'd otherwise be hidden

To avoid losing an existing choice, two people remain available in the picker even if the rules above would normally exclude them:

- the **default crew lead** carried over from the job's planning defaults, and
- anyone **already assigned** as the Team Lead on one of the days you're currently scheduling.

This means a previously chosen Team Lead won't silently disappear from the picker just because their role or branch changed.

<Note>
The "must be a Crew Leader" rule is applied in the **scheduling picker**. The picker is where eligibility is enforced — so make the assignment through the Team Lead field rather than expecting a separate check elsewhere.
</Note>

## Who can assign a crew lead

Assigning a crew lead happens when you **save or publish** a job's scheduled days. There is no separate "assign crew lead" permission — it is part of being allowed to edit the schedule.

### What it takes to assign

To set or change a Team Lead, you must:

1. Be able to **open the Schedule** (the scheduling area must be available to your role), and
2. Have permission to **edit the schedule** for that job, and
3. Be acting within **your own organization** and have **access to the job's branch**.

The branch check is the same scoping that governs the rest of the app — see [Org and branch scoping](/reference/platform/org-branch-scoping).

### Roles that can assign a crew lead

| Role | Can assign a crew lead? |
| --- | --- |
| Admin | Yes |
| Ops Manager | Yes |
| Sales Admin | Yes |
| Client Coordinator | Yes |
| Sales Member | Yes |
| Crew Leader | Yes |
| Crew Member | No |

A **Crew Leader** can therefore both *be* a Team Lead and *assign* one, because the Crew Leader role can open and edit the schedule. A **Crew Member** cannot assign a crew lead.

For the full role-by-permission breakdown, see the [Permissions and roles matrix](/reference/platform/permissions-roles-matrix).

### Who managers can act on

When a manager-level role opens the schedule, the people they can act on (including who they can set as Team Lead) span the team for that branch: Crew Member, Crew Leader, Sales Member, Ops Manager, Sales Admin, Client Coordinator, and Admin. People without a manager-level role only see themselves.

## Related job behavior

Setting a crew lead is part of staffing a job, which ties into the job's status:

- A job with no crew assigned sits in **Requires Crew Lead**.
- Assigning crew moves the job into **Plans In Progress**.
- Removing all crew from a job that isn't closed sends it back to **Requires Crew Lead**.

For the full set of job statuses and transitions, see [Job status lifecycle](/reference/jobs/job-status-lifecycle).

## Quick reference

- **The crew lead = the Team Lead on a scheduled day.** It's chosen per day and is optional.
- **To be picked:** must hold the **Crew Leader** role, belong to the job's branch, and be an active, non-placeholder member — with the default and already-assigned Team Lead always kept selectable.
- **To assign:** Admin, Ops Manager, Sales Admin, Client Coordinator, Sales Member, and Crew Leader can; **Crew Member** cannot.
- **A Crew Leader can both be and assign a Team Lead.**
