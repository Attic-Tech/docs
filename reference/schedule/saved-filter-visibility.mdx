This reference explains how saved Schedule filters work: who can see one, who can manage it (rename, re-share, overwrite, or delete), and how the "private" and "shared with branch" visibility levels behave.

The user-facing roles referenced below are: **Admin**, **Ops Manager**, **Sales Admin**, **Sales Member**, **Client Coordinator**, **Crew Leader**, and **Crew Member**.

## What a saved filter is

A saved Schedule filter is a named snapshot of your current Schedule page filters — the people, clients, roles, activity types, shift statuses, and branches you've selected. It belongs to a single branch and has one owner: the person who saved it.

### Two visibility levels

Every saved filter has exactly one of two visibility levels:

- **Private** — visible only to the person who created it.
- **Shared with branch** — visible to everyone working in the same branch.

There is no organization-wide or cross-branch visibility level. A filter is always scoped to the branch it was saved in.

## Saving a new filter

### Filters are private by default

In the **Save Current Filters** dialog, a new filter is **private unless you tick "Share with branch."** Ticking that box makes it shared with everyone in your branch; leaving it unticked keeps it private to you.

### A name is required

Every saved filter needs a name (up to 80 characters). The Save button won't accept an empty name.

## Who can see a saved filter

### You must belong to a branch

To work with saved Schedule filters at all, you must currently belong to a branch. If you don't, you'll see an error telling you that you must belong to a branch to manage saved Schedule filters.

### The visibility rule

Within your current branch, a saved filter shows up for you when **either**:

1. You own it, **or**
2. It is shared with the branch.

That means another person's **private** filter never appears in your list — and this does not change with your role. Even an Admin sees only their own filters plus the branch-shared ones in the picker. A private filter that belongs to someone else stays hidden.

If you try to open a specific filter you can't see (because it's private and not yours, or it's in a different branch), the system reports that the filter can't be found, rather than confirming it exists.

### Who can open Schedule and use saved filters

Using saved filters is part of Schedule access. The roles that can work in Schedule — **Admin**, **Ops Manager**, **Sales Admin**, **Sales Member**, **Client Coordinator**, and **Crew Leader** — can list the filters visible to them in their branch and save their own.

## Who can manage a saved filter

"Manage" means rename a filter, change its sharing, overwrite it with your current filters, or delete it.

### The management rule

Within your branch, you can manage a saved filter when **either**:

1. You are the **owner** of that filter, **or**
2. You are an **Admin**.

For everyone except Admin, management is **owner-only**: you can manage filters you created, and no one else's. Ops Manager, Sales Admin, Sales Member, Client Coordinator, Crew Leader, and Crew Member can all see branch-shared filters, but they cannot rename, re-share, overwrite, or delete a filter someone else created.

### What happens when you can't manage one

- **A branch-shared filter that isn't yours (and you're not an Admin):** you can see it, but you'll get a message that you don't have permission to modify it. Rename and delete controls don't appear on it.
- **Someone else's private filter:** the system reports it can't be found — a non-owner is never told that a private filter exists.

### Changing a filter's sharing

If you can manage a filter (you own it, or you're an Admin), you can flip it between **private** and **shared with branch** at any time. A filter's owner and branch, however, are fixed once it's created and can't be reassigned.

## Why the rename and delete buttons sometimes don't appear

In the saved-filters picker, the rename and delete controls show up only on filters you're allowed to manage — the ones you own, or (for an Admin) any filter. On a branch-shared filter created by a colleague, a non-Admin sees the filter but no rename or delete control, which matches the permission rule above.

The whole filter panel can also be disabled in some states; while it's disabled, the rename, delete, and default-star controls are hidden too.

<Note>
Even when a button is hidden, the permission rule is enforced when the action runs — the hidden controls are a convenience, not the boundary.
</Note>

## Setting a default filter is separate from sharing

Marking a saved filter as your default (the star) is a **personal preference**. It's stored just for you, per branch, and it does **not** change who can see the filter. One person's default never affects anyone else, and the star appears on any filter you can see — there's no management permission required to set your own default.

## Quick reference

- **Default visibility of a new filter:** private, unless you tick **Share with branch**.
- **See a filter:** the owner, plus everyone in the branch if it's shared with the branch. Others' private filters are always hidden.
- **Manage a filter (rename / re-share / overwrite / delete):** the owner, or an Admin. Everyone else is owner-only.
- **A branch-shared filter you don't own:** visible, but a non-Admin can't rename or delete it.
- **Setting a default:** a personal, per-branch preference — it does not share the filter.
