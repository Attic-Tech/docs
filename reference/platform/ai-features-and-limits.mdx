This reference explains Menaia's AI-assisted features, the limits on what you can submit to each one, and the rules that keep AI output a draft you review rather than something applied automatically.

## What the AI features are

Menaia's AI helpers fall into two groups:

- **Drafting and writing helpers** that propose text you then review and edit — crew notes, the proposal email, and win celebration posts.
- **Analysis helpers** that summarize a recorded sales conversation — ride-along insights.

### Nothing the AI produces is applied automatically

Every AI feature returns a **suggestion**. The result is never sent, saved, or applied on its own. The screen always drops the output into an editable field, an undo-able replacement, or a review step first. See [Always review AI output](#always-review-ai-output) below.

## What each feature does

### Crew notes (Enhance / Suggest)

On the inspection-report step, the crew-notes field has an in-field AI button that rewrites your notes into a concise sales-to-crew heads-up.

- There are two modes: **Enhance with AI** when you've already typed notes, and **Suggest with AI** when the field is empty (it then drafts from the job context — customer demeanor, concerns, and special instructions).
- The result replaces the field but is **fully undo-able**. Your prior text is kept, and an Undo / Revert control restores it.

### Proposal email (Draft / Enhance)

On a proposal, the email body field has an AI button that rewrites the body into a warm, professional client-facing email (or writes a default body if the field is empty).

- The AI is told **not to invent pricing, dates, attachments, guarantees, or legal terms**, and to keep your factual claims intact.
- The result is undo-able — your prior body is kept before the new text is applied.

### Win celebration post

In the feed composer, **Use AI** opens a three-step flow that drafts a celebration post from an existing win (a signed proposal, hours saved, or a badge unlocked) and tags the people involved.

- The steps are: 1) pick a recent win, 2) add an optional note or GIF, 3) **Review & Post** — the generated text lands in an editor so you can edit it before sharing.
- You can **Regenerate** on the review step if you don't like the draft.
- The AI is told **not to invent details that weren't provided**.

### Ride-along insights

The **AI Ride Along** recorder captures an in-person sales conversation. After you stop, the audio is transcribed and analyzed, and results appear as tabbed insights.

- The recorder explains: "Record your in-person sales conversation. When you're done, AI will analyze the conversation and provide coaching insights."
- The analysis is organized into sections: **Summary**, **Strengths**, **Next Steps** (or **Follow-up** if the deal closed on the spot), **Coaching** insights, **Tone** and communication analysis (talk-time percentage, confidence, engagement, hesitations), **Patterns**, and ready-to-send **Drafts** for a follow-up email or text.
- Per-speaker tone, confidence, and engagement come from voice analysis. When that voice data isn't available, those fields are shown as empty rather than guessed.

## Usage limits

### Length and size limits

| Feature | Limit |
| --- | --- |
| Crew notes | Up to 5,000 characters (input is capped, and AI output is held to the same limit) |
| Proposal email body | Up to 6,000 characters (input capped, output held to the same limit) |
| Proposal email subject context | Up to 300 characters |
| Win-post note | Up to 500 characters |
| Ride-along recording | Auto-stops at 2 hours |

### Processing time for ride-alongs

- Ride-along analysis "usually takes 30 to 90 seconds. You can navigate away — the analysis will continue in the background."
- The results screen checks for completion automatically and stops checking after roughly 15 minutes, then shows "Still processing — check back soon … Refresh the page in a few minutes to check on the analysis."
- If analysis can't complete you'll see **Analysis Failed** with the reason, or "An unexpected error occurred during analysis."

## Who can use each AI feature

AI features are turned on per workspace and, in some cases, depend on your role, so not every workspace or user sees them.

- **Crew notes and the proposal email helper** appear where you can edit the underlying record — the inspection-report step and the proposal email, respectively.
- **Ride-Alongs** must be turned on for your workspace. The voice tone analysis depends on an additional voice-analysis setting being turned on for your workspace.
- **AI win posts** require win channels to be turned on. With them off, you'll see "This feature is not available for your organization."

If an AI feature you expect is missing, it's almost always because the feature isn't turned on for your workspace, or your role can't edit the underlying record. See [Permissions and roles](/reference/platform/permissions-roles-matrix).

## Always review AI output

AI output is a draft to check, not a finished artifact — and this is enforced by the interface, not just advised.

### Text replacements are undo-able

Crew-notes and proposal-email replacements can be reverted (Keep / Revert), so you can compare against your original and roll back.

### Win posts open in a review step

A win post opens in an editor on a dedicated **Review & Post** step (with **Regenerate**) before anything is shared to the feed.

### The AI is constrained against making things up

- Proposal emails must **not invent pricing, dates, guarantees, or legal terms**.
- Win posts must **not invent details** that weren't provided.

### Customer-typed text is treated as untrusted

All AI features treat user-typed text as content to summarize, not as instructions to follow — so odd phrasing in a customer's notes can't redirect the AI. This is worth knowing if a customer's notes contain unusual wording.
